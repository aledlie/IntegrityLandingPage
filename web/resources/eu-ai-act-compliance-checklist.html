EU AI Act Compliance Checklist

Complete checklist for high-risk AI systems covering Articles 9-15 requirements. Track your compliance progress and identify gaps before the August 2026 deadline.

Key Compliance Deadlines

The EU AI Act entered into force on August 1, 2024. Here are the critical dates:

  • February 2, 2025: Prohibited AI practices become enforceable
  • August 2, 2025: GPAI (General Purpose AI) rules apply
  • August 2, 2026: High-risk AI system requirements enforceable
  • August 2, 2027: Remaining provisions fully applicable

Risk Management System

Article 9

Establish and maintain a risk management system as an iterative process running throughout the entire lifecycle of the AI system.

Risk Identification & Analysis
  • Identified and analyzed known and reasonably foreseeable risksCritical
    Document risks to health, safety, and fundamental rights when system is used as intended
  • Estimated and evaluated risks from reasonably foreseeable misuseCritical
    Consider how the system might be misused and potential harms from misuse
  • Evaluated risks based on post-market monitoring dataHigh
    Incorporate learnings from deployed systems and incident reports
Risk Mitigation
  • Adopted appropriate risk management measuresCritical
    Implement controls to eliminate or reduce identified risks to acceptable levels
  • Considered technical knowledge, experience, and state of the artHigh
    Risk measures should reflect current best practices and technological capabilities
  • Documented residual risks with justificationHigh
    For risks that cannot be eliminated, document why they are acceptable
Testing & Validation
  • Performed appropriate testing proceduresCritical
    Test against clearly defined metrics and probabilistic thresholds
  • Tested in real-world conditions where applicableHigh
    Validate performance in conditions matching intended deployment environment
2

Data and Data Governance

Article 10

Ensure training, validation, and testing datasets meet quality standards appropriate for the system's intended purpose.

Dataset Quality
  • Training data is relevant to intended purposeCritical
    Data should be appropriate and sufficient for the system's use case
  • Datasets are sufficiently representativeCritical
    Cover the diversity of persons, groups, and settings where system will be used
  • Datasets are free of errors to the best extent possibleHigh
    Implement data cleaning and quality assurance processes
  • Datasets are complete for the intended purposeHigh
    No critical gaps that could affect system performance or safety
Bias Management
  • Examined datasets for possible biasesCritical
    Assess for biases related to protected characteristics and vulnerable groups
  • Implemented measures to detect and address biasesCritical
    Document bias detection methods and mitigation strategies applied
  • Considered geographic, contextual, and behavioral biasesHigh
    Account for biases that may arise from data collection circumstances
Data Governance Practices
  • Documented data collection processesHigh
    Maintain records of data sources, collection methods, and processing steps
  • Established data management proceduresHigh
    Define processes for data storage, access control, and lifecycle management
3

Technical Documentation

Article 11

Prepare comprehensive technical documentation demonstrating compliance before placing the system on the market.

System Description
  • Documented general description of the AI systemCritical
    Include intended purpose, developer information, and system version
  • Described elements and development processCritical
    Algorithms, data, training process, and key design choices
  • Documented system architecture and componentsHigh
    Hardware, software, and data flow diagrams
Performance & Compliance
  • Documented performance metrics and benchmarksCritical
    Accuracy, robustness, and other relevant performance measures
  • Included validation and testing resultsCritical
    Test methodologies, datasets used, and outcomes
  • Documented risk management measuresHigh
    Reference to risk assessment and mitigation strategies
Documentation Management
  • Documentation is kept up to dateHigh
    Update when substantial modifications are made to the system
  • Retained documentation for at least 10 yearsCritical
    From date system is placed on market or put into service
4

Record-Keeping (Logging)

Article 12

Design systems with automatic logging capabilities to ensure traceability throughout the system's lifetime.

Automatic Logging Capabilities
  • System automatically records events during operationCritical
    Logging must be automatic without requiring manual intervention
  • Logs enable identification of risk situationsCritical
    Capture events that may indicate system presenting risk or substantial modification
  • Logs facilitate post-market monitoring (Article 72)Critical
    Support ongoing surveillance after deployment
  • Logs support operational monitoring (Article 26(5))Critical
    Enable deployers to monitor system operation
Log Content Requirements
  • Logs include timestamps (start and end of sessions)Critical
    ISO 8601 format with timezone for unambiguous timing
  • Logs record input data references (hashed for PII protection)Critical
    Enable traceability without exposing personal data
  • Logs capture output/decision informationCritical
    Record what the system decided or produced
  • Logs identify human verifiers (where applicable)High
    For biometric systems, record identity of persons verifying results
Log Storage & Security
  • Logs maintained throughout system lifetimeCritical
    Retention policies aligned with system lifecycle and legal requirements
  • Logs stored securely with access controlsHigh
    Implement appropriate security measures per Article 15
  • Logs are tamper-evident (recommended)Medium
    Consider hash chains or similar integrity mechanisms
5

Transparency and Information

Article 13

Design systems to be sufficiently transparent to enable deployers to interpret outputs and use the system appropriately.

Instructions for Use
  • Provided clear instructions for deployersCritical
    Concise, complete, correct, and accessible documentation
  • Documented intended purpose and limitationsCritical
    Clear statement of what system is designed to do and known limitations
  • Included performance characteristicsCritical
    Accuracy levels, confidence thresholds, known failure modes
  • Specified hardware and software requirementsHigh
    Computational resources, dependencies, and integration requirements
Transparency Information
  • Explained how to interpret system outputsCritical
    Guidance on understanding and acting on AI decisions
  • Documented human oversight measuresHigh
    How humans can monitor, interpret, and intervene
  • Disclosed risks to vulnerable groups (if applicable)High
    Special considerations for children, disabled persons, or other vulnerable groups
6

Human Oversight

Article 14

Design systems to allow effective oversight by humans during the period of use.

Oversight Capabilities
  • Humans can understand system capabilities and limitationsCritical
    Design enables human comprehension of what system can/cannot do
  • Humans can monitor system operationCritical
    Real-time or near-real-time visibility into system behavior
  • Humans can interpret system outputs correctlyCritical
    Outputs presented in ways that enable proper interpretation
Intervention Capabilities
  • Humans can decide not to use system outputCritical
    Ability to disregard, override, or reverse AI decisions
  • Humans can intervene in system operationCritical
    Mechanisms to pause, stop, or modify system behavior
  • Emergency stop functionality availableHigh
    For systems with physical effects, ability to immediately halt operation
Automation Bias Prevention
  • Measures to prevent over-reliance on AI outputsHigh
    Design discourages blind acceptance of AI decisions
  • Training provided for human overseersHigh
    Personnel trained on system capabilities, limitations, and oversight procedures
7

Accuracy, Robustness, and Cybersecurity

Article 15

Design systems to achieve appropriate levels of accuracy, robustness, and cybersecurity throughout their lifecycle.

Accuracy
  • System achieves appropriate accuracy levelsCritical
    Accuracy consistent with intended purpose and state of the art
  • Accuracy metrics documented and communicatedCritical
    Clear documentation of accuracy levels in instructions for use
  • Accuracy maintained over system lifecycleHigh
    Monitoring to detect and address accuracy degradation
Robustness
  • System resilient to errors and inconsistenciesCritical
    Handles edge cases and unexpected inputs gracefully
  • System maintains performance under various conditionsHigh
    Testing across diverse operating conditions and data distributions
  • Fail-safe mechanisms implementedHigh
    System fails safely when encountering problems
Cybersecurity
  • Resilient against unauthorized third-party accessCritical
    Protection against exploitation of system vulnerabilities
  • Protected against adversarial attacksCritical
    Measures against data poisoning, model manipulation, input attacks
  • Security measures appropriate to risksHigh
    Security controls proportionate to identified risks
  • Regular security assessments conductedHigh
    Ongoing vulnerability assessments and penetration testing
8

Registration and Conformity

Articles 16, 49

Register the AI system and complete conformity assessment before placing on the market.

EU Database Registration
  • Registered in EU database before market placementCritical
    Complete registration before placing system on market or putting into service
  • Registration information is accurate and completeCritical
    All required fields completed with correct information
  • Registration kept up to dateHigh
    Update registration when system details change
Conformity Assessment
  • Completed appropriate conformity assessmentCritical
    Self-assessment or third-party assessment as required
  • Applied CE markingCritical
    CE marking affixed after successful conformity assessment
  • EU Declaration of Conformity preparedCritical
    Signed declaration stating system meets all requirements
Quality Management
  • Quality management system implementedCritical
    Systematic approach to ensuring ongoing compliance
  • Post-market monitoring system establishedCritical
    Active monitoring of system performance after deployment
  • Corrective action procedures definedHigh
    Process for addressing non-conformities and incidents
